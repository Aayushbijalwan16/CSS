Gulab Jamun

Ingredients
  1. 1. For the syrup
  2. 2. 4 green cardamom pods, lightly squashed with the side of a knife
  3. 3. 600g caster sugar
  4. 4. few drops rosewater
  5. 5. ¼ tsp saffron strands, soaked in 2 tbsp warm water for 1 hr
  6. 6. 2 tsp chopped pistachios
  7. 7. For the gulab jamun
  8. 8. 6 green cardamom pods, seeds removed
  9. 9. 1 tsp caster sugar
  10. 10. 100g full-cream milk powder
  11. 11. 40g plain flour
  12. 12. ½ tsp baking powder
  13. 13. 50g ghee or softened unsalted butter plus 1kg ghee, for frying
  14. 14. 1 tbsp natural yogurt
  15. 15. squeeze of lemon juice
  16. 16. 4-5 tbsp whole milk sunflower oil
Method
  1. 1. To make the syrup, gently heat the cardamom, sugar and 500ml water in a saucepan. Don't let the water boil before the sugar has dissolved. Simmer for 10 mins, then cool.
  2. 2. Gradually add the rosewater, saffron and a little of its soaking liquid until you have a subtly flavoured syrup. Set aside.
  3. 3. To make the gulab jamun, use a pestle and mortar to grind together the cardamom and sugar until fine.
  4. 4. Sieve the milk powder, flour and baking powder into a mixing bowl and stir in the cardamom and sugar mixture. Rub in the 50g ghee or butter using your fingertips until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  5. 5. Add the yogurt, lemon juice and enough milk to form a soft dough, taking care not to overwork the mixture.
  6. 6. Oil your hands with a little sunflower oil and shape small portions of the dough into walnut-sized balls, about 3cm in diameter and 20g each. Make sure the dumplings are smooth and have no cracks or folds.
  7. 7. Reheat the syrup until it comes to the boil, then turn off the heat and cover with a lid.
  8. 8. Heat the 1kg ghee in a wok or karahi to 130C or until a small piece of dough dropped in turns golden in 40 seconds. Fry the balls, in batches, over a medium heat for 5-7 mins, moving them around until they are an even, deep brown. Remove from the saucepan using a slotted spoon, then drain on kitchen paper. Add to the warm syrup, leaving them to soak for 2-3 hrs or preferably overnight.
  9. 9. When ready to eat, warm the gulab jamun through in a wide saucepan, spooning over the syrup. Serve piping hot with the pistachios scattered over.

Bessan ladu

INGREDIENTS
  1. 1. ½ cup ghee / clarified butter
  2. 2. 2 cup besan / gram flour, coarse
  3. 3. 1 cup sugar
  4. 4. 4 pods cardamom / elachi
  5. 5. 2 tbsp melon seeds
  6. 6. 2 tbsp cashew / kaju, chopped
Method:-
  1. 1. in a large kadai heat ½ cup ghee and add 2 cup besan.
  2. 2. roast on low flame until the besan is well combined with ghee. make sure to use coarse besan for grainy texture.
  3. 3. continue to roast on low flame. if the mixture turns dry, add a tbsp of more ghee.
  4. 4. after 20 minutes, the besan starts to release ghee.
  5. 5. continue to roast until the besan turns golden brown and grainy. it may take approximately 30 minutes.
  6. 6. transfer the mixture to a large bowl, allowing to cool slightly.
  7. 7. meanwhile, dry roast 2 tbsp melon seeds and 2 tbsp cashew.
  8. 8. roast on low flame until the nuts turn crunchy.
  9. 9. add the roasted nuts to roasted besan ghee mixture.
  10. 10. take 1 cup sugar and 4 pods cardamom in a blender. you can alternatively use tagar or boora.
  11. 11. blend to a fine powder without adding any water.
  12. 12. once the besan is cooled (slightly warm) add in powdered sugar.
  13. 13. mix well making sure everything is well combined. do not add sugar if the mixture is hot, as it will melt the sugar and makes mixture watery.
  14. 14. prepare a ball sized ladoo adjusting sugar as required.
  15. 15. finally, enjoy besan ladoo for 2 weeks in an airtight container.

Kadhai Paneer

Ingredients
  1. 1. Paneer (cottage cheese) 200 grams
  2. 2. Fennel seeds (saunf) 1 teaspoon
  3. 3. Cumin seeds 2 tablespoons
  4. 4. Coriander seeds 3 tablespoons
  5. 5. Black peppercorns 1/2 teaspoons
  6. 6. Kashmiri red chillies dried 4
  7. 7. Oil 2-3 tablespoons
  8. 8. Tomatoes chopped 6 medium
  9. 9. Kashmiri red chilli powder 1 teaspoon
  10. 10. Salt to taste
  11. 11. Ginger cut into juliennes 1 inch
  12. 12. Onion cut into cubes 1/2 medium
  13. 13. Green capsicum 1/2 medium
  14. 14. Tomato 1 medium
  15. 15. Garam masala powder 1 teaspoon
  16. 16. Dried fenugreek leaves (kasoori methi) 1 teaspoon
Method:-
  1. 1. To make kadai powder, dry roast 2 red chillies, fennel seeds, 1 teaspoon cumin seeds, 2 tablespoons coriander seeds, peppercorns till fragrant. Cool and grind into a coarse powder.
  2. 2. Heat oil in a non-stick pan. Add remaining cumin seeds and red chillies and sauté till seeds crackle.
  3. 3. Add remaining coriander seeds, tomatoes, mix and sauté for 5-6 minutes or till tomatoes are pulpy.
  4. 4. Cut cottage cheese into cubes.
  5. 5. Add 1 tablespoon kadai powder and mix well. Add onion cubes and capsicum cubes.
  6. 6. Cut tomato into cubes and add. Mix well and add cottage cheese cubes. Toss to mix and cook for a minute.
  7. 7. Add garam masala powder, toss to mix and cook for ½ a minute. Add dried fenugreek leaves and toss to mix.
  8. 8. Sprinkle 1 tablespoon kadai powder on top and serve hot.

Palak Paneer

Ingredients
  1. 1. 500g/1lb 2oz spinach
  2. 2. 500ml/18fl oz boiling water
  3. 3. 3 tsp cornmeal or fine polenta
  4. 4. 4 tsp ghee or 6 tsp sunflower or rapeseed oil
  5. 5. 400g/14oz paneer, cut into chunks
  6. 6. 1 tsp cumin seeds
  7. 7. 20g/¾oz fresh root ginger, peeled and grated
  8. 8. 20g/¾oz garlic, finely chopped
  9. 9. 125g/4½oz onion, finely chopped
  10. 10. 2–3 small green chillies, seeds in (bird’s eye or similar), chopped
  11. 11. 1 tsp ground turmeric
  12. 12. 2 tsp garam masala
  13. 13. 1 tsp salt
Method
  1. 1.Tip the spinach into a saucepan and pour over the water. Bring to the boil and cook for 4–5 minutes until tender. Drain, reserving 50ml/2fl oz of the cooking water. Transfer the cooked spinach to a bowl.
  2. 2. Add the cornmeal to the cooked spinach, along with the reserved water. Blend to a smooth purée with a stick blender. Set aside.
  3. 3. Heat 2 teaspoons of the ghee or oil in pan over a medium heat, then fry the paneer chunks until evenly browned on all sides. Transfer to a plate and set aside.
  4. 4. In the same pan, heat the remaining ghee or oil, then add the cumin seeds. As soon as they begin to sizzle, add the ginger and garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add the onion and chillies and cook for another 7–8 minutes on a medium heat until the onions are softened.
  5. 5. Stir in the turmeric, garam masala and salt and cook for 1 minute more. Stir in the puréed spinach, lower the heat and cover the pan. Cook for another 4 minutes, then add the cooked paneer and cook for a further 2 minutes.
  6. 6. Serve hot with a flatbread or plain rice

Gajar Halwa

Ingredients
  1. 1. 1 kg Carrots , grated (for best results, try and avoid using the core as much as possible)
  2. 2. 4 tablespoons Ghee
  3. 3. 1 liter Milk
  4. 4. 3/4 cup Sugar
  5. 5. 1 teaspoon Cardamom powder
  6. 6. 1/2 cup Whole Almonds (Badam) , blanched and cut lengthwise
  7. 7. 2 pinch Saffron strands
Method:-
  1. 1. To begin making the Gajjar Halwa Recipe, make sure you have all the ingredients ready and kept by the side. Grate the carrots using a hand grater or a food processor.
  2. 2. Heat 2 tablespoons ghee in a heavy bottomed pan; add the carrots and sauté for a few minutes until the carrots become lightly soft.
  3. 3. Add the milk and turn the heat to medium high and allow the milk and carrot mixture to come to a boil. Once the milk and carrot mixture come to a boil, turn the heat to medium low and cook the gajjar halwa until entire milk is evaporated.
  4. 4. This process will take close to 35 to 40 minutes. Make sure you keep stirring the Gajjar halwa with a wooden spoon every time the milk froths up. Stirring also prevents the halwa from sticking to the bottom of the pan.
  5. 5. Half way through this process, add the sugar and cook and stir until the entire milk is evaporated and until the Gajjar halwa mixture is thickened.
  6. 6. When you notice a halwa like consistency, add the remaining ghee, cardamom powder, saffron and the sliced almonds. Simmer for another 5 minutes and turn off the heat.
  7. 7. Transfer the Gajjar Halwa to a serving bowl and serve hot or cold for a delicious dessert after a meal.
  8. 8. You can serve the Gajjar Halwa for the cold winter nights, topped with vanilla ice cream or on its own. Serve Gajjar Ka Halwa Recipe as a dessert after your delicious festive meal of Jodhpuri Aloo Ki Sabzi, Ajwain Puri and Boondi Raita.
